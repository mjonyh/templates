In this thesis, we investigate the structural, electronic, and optical properties of advanced semiconductor materials using first-principles calculations based on Density Functional Theory (DFT). The calculations are performed using the projector augmented-wave method as implemented in the Quantum ESPRESSO and VASP packages. The exchange-correlation functional is treated with both local and semi-local approximations, and extended with hybrid functional calculations to accurately predict bandgap properties. Our results reveal significant tuning of electronic states under biaxial strain, paving the way for applications in next-generation optoelectronic devices.
