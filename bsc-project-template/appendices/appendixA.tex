\section{Gaussian Beam Radius Derivation}
The radius $w(z)$ of a Gaussian laser beam propagating along the $z$-axis is given by:
\begin{equation}
    w(z) = w_0 \sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{z}{z_0}\right)^2}
    \label{eq:beam_radius}
\end{equation}
where $w_0$ is the beam waist radius and $z_0 = \frac{\pi w_0^2}{\lambda}$ is the Rayleigh range.

The peak intensity $I(z)$ at a given distance $z$ is:
\begin{equation}
    I(z) = I_0 \left( \frac{w_0}{w(z)} \right)^2 = \frac{I_0}{1 + \left(\frac{z}{z_0}\right)^2}
    \label{eq:intensity_distribution}
\end{equation}
where $I_0$ represents peak intensity at the focus ($z=0$). These equations are used to model the transmission coordinates plotted in Chapter \ref{ch:results}.
